# Sample Deck Signals

These notes summarize recurring content signals observed across reviewed Nexon customer-facing monthly report decks.

## Shared cross-deck patterns

- cover slide with month and presenter
- agenda slide with customer-specific themes
- operational sections separated by clear divider slides or headline resets
- summary slides before detailed tables
- commentary blocks attached to charts and tables
- appendix or follow-on detail where tables become long

## Shared reporting behaviors

- the decks are not one fixed universal template
- they still preserve a recognizable reporting backbone
- the best slides connect posture, evidence, and interpretation
- the strongest decks show trends and exceptions rather than only totals

## Operations-heavy deck signals

Observed themes:

- services review
- primary availability and site uptime
- incident and request review
- SLA response and resolution
- detailed breach tables
- DaaS and survey review
- monitoring alerts

Implication for the combined skill:

- operations-first reports need strong service desk, SLA, observability, and appendix detail

## Governance-and-security-heavy deck signals

Observed themes:

- open action register
- open projects
- vulnerability management
- service desk calls
- network summary
- incident and services review
- risks, issues, and planning

Implication for the combined skill:

- governance and security modules often deserve first-class optional treatment rather than being treated as afterthought appendices

## Security-and-lifecycle-heavy deck signals

Observed themes:

- SOC overview
- security event reporting
- onboarding and offboarding
- service desk call statistics
- monthly commentary
- incident and request review
- DaaS management
- problem and change management

Implication for the combined skill:

- security-first and lifecycle-heavy customers need combined decks that blend operational and security sections smoothly

## Governance-and-business-context deck signals

Observed themes:

- open actions
- commercials
- user summary
- secure score
- services review
- resource availability
- service desk call statistics
- risks, issues, and planning

Implication for the combined skill:

- combined reports may need manual or semi-manual business and governance sections alongside source-driven operational evidence

## What the sample decks suggest

- reviewed Nexon monthly reports already set the bar for visual and structural quality
- combined reports should not be reduced to a short executive memo
- appendix depth is expected and acceptable when it supports the main story
- commentary is part of the value, not decoration

## What not to copy blindly

- another customer's exact agenda
- another customer's section ordering when the service scope differs
- manual sections that have no equivalent for the current customer
- raw table volume without summary framing
