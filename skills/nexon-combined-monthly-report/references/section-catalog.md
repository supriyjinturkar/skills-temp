# Section Catalog

Use this catalog as a menu of helpful module options for a combined Nexon monthly customer report.

Do not force every module into every report. Select modules based on:

- customer service scope
- the sources available this month
- the strongest operational signals in the period
- the amount of customer action or visibility required

## 1. Common opening set

These are common starting modules for many combined monthly reports:

1. Cover
2. Agenda
3. Executive summary

### Cover

Include:

- customer name
- report family
- reporting month or period label
- presenter or service owner line when required

### Agenda

Include only the modules actually present in the report.

Good agenda themes:

- executive posture
- service desk performance
- incident and backlog health
- infrastructure and monitoring
- backup and protection
- security and governance
- actions and appendix

### Executive summary

Purpose:

- give the customer a fast, decision-ready view of the month

Include:

- 4 to 8 KPI cards
- 3 to 6 key observations
- 2 to 5 action or watch items when relevant

Good input areas:

- ticket volume
- SLA posture
- open backlog risk
- monitoring availability
- backup success
- critical incidents or aged risks

## 2. Common operational modules

These are reusable combined-report modules. Use the ones that fit the customer and month.

### Service desk overview

Use when ServiceNow or equivalent service-desk data is available.

Include:

- ticket volume by class
- opened versus closed movement
- priority or severity composition
- assignment group or service dimension signal
- short commentary on trend movement and workload posture

### SLA performance

Use when response and resolution commitments are available.

Include:

- response SLA posture
- resolution SLA posture
- overall SLA posture
- breach counts
- top causes or patterns when known
- breach detail moved to appendix when long

### Incident and backlog health

Use when open ticket age, priority, or unresolved workload is important.

Include:

- aged backlog
- open incident/request profile
- highest-risk age bucket
- ownership or dependency signal when known

### Change and problem management

Use when change or problem work is meaningful for the customer.

Include:

- monthly change totals
- notable open or high-risk changes
- open problems or recurring root-cause themes
- action-focused commentary

### Infrastructure and monitoring

Use when LogicMonitor or similar observability data exists.

Include:

- monitored estate summary
- availability posture
- alert trend
- active critical or degraded resources
- monitoring coverage caveats

### Backup and data protection

Use when BackupRadar or similar backup data exists.

Include:

- backup success posture
- protected devices or jobs
- failed, warning, retried, or pending trend
- notable backup exceptions

## 3. Optional specialized modules

These appear only when the customer scope or month justifies them.

### Security posture

Use when the customer receives SOC, Secure Score, firewall, or security-event reporting.

Include:

- high-level security posture
- notable events or advisories
- response or acknowledgement performance if tracked
- top risks or mitigation themes

### Vulnerability management

Use when vulnerability findings are part of the customer-facing service.

Include:

- overall findings posture
- discovered versus remediated trend
- top immediate risks
- remediation-in-progress view

### User and lifecycle activity

Use when onboarding, offboarding, DaaS, or hardware lifecycle work matters.

Include:

- onboarding/offboarding counts
- lifecycle workload
- notable delays, approvals, or blockers

### Commercials, projects, and planning

Use when the report includes renewals, open projects, commercials, or planning items.

Include:

- open action register
- open projects
- renewals or commercials summary
- risks, issues, dependencies, and next-step owners

### Satisfaction, survey, or call-center modules

Use when the customer expects service desk call statistics, CSAT, or survey reporting.

Include:

- monthly call or survey totals
- answer or abandonment posture if available
- wait-time or quality signal
- short customer-impact commentary

## 4. Appendix module set

Move these to appendix by default unless they are the core issue of the month:

- detailed SLA breach lists
- aged incident tables
- top alert tables
- detailed backup exception rows
- raw dimension breakdowns
- patching, AV, storage, inventory, or asset extracts
- supporting methodology notes

## 5. Module selection hints

### Operations-first report

Prioritize:

- executive summary
- service desk overview
- SLA performance
- incident and backlog health
- infrastructure and monitoring
- backup and data protection
- recommendations and actions

### Security-first report

Prioritize:

- executive summary
- security posture
- vulnerability management
- incident and backlog health
- service desk overview
- recommendations and actions

### Governance-heavy report

Prioritize:

- executive summary
- open actions and projects
- commercials or planning
- service desk overview
- risk, issues, and forward plan
- appendix evidence

## 6. Scope guidance

Many well-formed combined monthly reports end up with something like:

- 1 opening set
- 3 to 6 main modules
- 1 recommendations or actions section when needed
- 1 appendix divider and supporting appendix content

Treat that as a rough shape, not a hard rule.
