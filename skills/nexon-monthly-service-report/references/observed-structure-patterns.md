# Observed Structure Patterns

These notes summarize recurring report-flow behavior from the deidentified Nexon monthly decks under `.vscode/`.

## Repeated Narrative Rhythm

1. Cover slide.
2. Agenda slide.
3. One to three framing/setup slides where needed.
4. Main operational sections introduced with clear resets/dividers.
5. Summary-first slides before detail-heavy slides.
6. Appendix or support material separated from the core story.

## Recurring Evidence Patterns

- High-level KPI or posture slides appear before raw operational detail.
- Exception-oriented commentary is common on:
  - SLA breach slides
  - priority incident slides
  - backlog or aged-item slides
  - month-over-month trend slides
- Detail often continues onto follow-on slides instead of being compressed into a single overloaded page.

## What Makes The Decks Useful

- They show movement, not just totals.
- They connect summary statements to visible evidence.
- They surface items needing attention instead of presenting volume alone.
- They keep heavy supporting material available without letting it dominate the story.

## What To Avoid

- Copying one customer's exact agenda into every deck.
- Starting sections with raw dumps before the audience knows the posture.
- Hiding important caveats or exceptions in dense notes.
- Filling slides with narrative when a short commentary block will do.
